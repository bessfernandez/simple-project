Lauren Hough

  • I am an educator who supports individuals with social cognitive differences.
  • I see the limitations in the "one-size-fits-all" model for all of the players on the board.
  • I believe that we can forge partnerships, honoring each individual's unique strengths.
  • I hope to bridge the gap between different players and, together, redesign the board.

Susan Brennan

  • I am an SLP and educator on a quest to find a common language.
  • I see the strength in building a bridge between neurotypical & neurodiverse perspectives.
  • I believe in the Middleground: where we each strive to listen to and share our unique thinking, desires and strengths.
  • I hope to foster a collaboration with autistic individuals that informs a more mindful therapeutic practice.

Aaron Lanou

  • I am an educator and staff developer helping to support students on the spectrum.
  • I see an exclusion of critical voices in the discussion about understanding people with autism.
  • I believe the inclusion of all perspectives will lead to a truer and fairer understanding of human differences.
  • I hope to work toward a deeper understanding of autism to create better environments and available supports.

Kristie Patten Koenig

  • I am an educator, occupational therapist and researcher who has shifted my paradigm from a focus on identifying deficits to strength-based.
  • I see that we are missing an opportunity to partner by focusing on the professional as expert.
  • I believe that no one builds lives on remediated weaknesses, so why is that our focus?
  • I hope to forge a new partnership with autistic individuals to create a national model to fundamentally change research, services and education.
